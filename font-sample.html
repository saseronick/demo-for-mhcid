Forty Corners

Forty corners
for measly quarters,
I grumble as I head home.

Fingers numb
and fumbling from
my growing frown.

Fleshy fog fills in
wholes, making fuzzy frontiers.
Shaley centers.

Listing forward
we ration nightly,
wishing daddy were brave.

And fluidly I linger,
awaiting the longest note,
toes at the last door.
